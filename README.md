## 🔍 Section 1: The Problem with Retail Forex Market Making

For decades, retail Forex has been dominated by CFD brokers. In this model, it is structurally impossible for a retail trader to be a true market maker:
- **Fixed Spreads**: Brokers set the spread, not the market.
- **B-Book Execution**: You are trading against the broker, not providing liquidity to a central book.
- **Directional Risk**: You are forced to take a directional bet, as you cannot capture the spread as a liquidity provider.

**The "House Always Wins" Trap:**
In a CFD environment, the broker is your counterparty. If you win, they lose. This creates a conflict of interest where brokers can manipulate spreads or execution times to their advantage. True market making requires a **Neutral Exchange** where you compete with other participants, not the house.

The future is tokenized. RWAs (tokenized Apple stocks, US Treasury bonds, etc.) are the next big wave in DeFi. The biggest challenge for RWA adoption is **liquidity**. Those who provide this liquidity early will reap disproportionate rewards.

**Why RWAs are Different:**
